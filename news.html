"I don't think I've ever felt like an underdog at Twickenham," the Harlequins prop said. "Instead, I look at it and think 'I know how good Ireland are. I know how good their tight-heads are. 'Melted wheelie bin' Furlong The job of England's front row will be made particularly difficult by talented Ireland prop Tadhg Furlong. Furlong is as fleet of foot as he is forceful up front and Marler is well aware of the challenge ahead. "He has got the ability to anchor a scrum with his massive chest, his massive weight and do that over and over to give his side a platform to play off really quick ball," Marler said. "He's got the ability to do that but then get up and stand in an attacking position. "He's playing off 10 [taking passes from the Ireland fly-half], yet he looks like he shouldn't be able to do that. He looks like a very traditional front-rower - a melted wheelie bin a bit like me."
